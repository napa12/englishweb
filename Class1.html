GRAMMAR POINTS

Basic form

The present perfect simple is subject + have/has + past participle. The past participle often ends in -ed (finished/decided etc.), but many verbs are irregular (lost/done/written etc.).

Basic usage

We use the present perfect when we do not have a specific time for the actions that occured:

- without a reference of time:

  • She has typed the letters.
  • They've bought a new car.
  • Have you passed the exam?
  • I have finished my homework.
  • She has traveled to many countries.
  • They have eaten sushi before.
  • He has read that book.
  • We have seen this movie.
  • The team has won several championships.
  • They have visited the Grand Canyon.
  • He has lived in this city for a long time.
  • We have cleaned the house.
  • She has cooked dinner for us.
  • They have painted the walls blue.
  • He has fixed the broken window.
  • I have met him once before.
  • She has run a marathon.
  • We have completed the project.
  • They have bought a new car.
  • I have spoken to her on the phone.

- with a reference of time:

just: acabou de acontecer

  • I've just got home.
  • They've just left the classroom.
  • She's just prepared the lunch.
  • We've just bought new clothes.

never: nunca aconteceu

  • I've seen a unicorn.
  • They've never been to Europe.
  • She's never prepared lunch.
  • We've never bought a house.

ever: aconteceu alguma vez

  • Have I ever seen you?
  • Have they ever been to Europe?
  • Has she ever prepared lunch?
  • Have we ever bought a house?

yet: ainda não aconteceu(negativa)

  • I haven't seen the film yet.
  • They haven't been to Europe yet.
  • She hasn't prepared the lunch yet.
  • We haven't bought a house yet.

yet, already: já aconteceu(yet, interrogativas; already, em afirmativas)

  • Have I seen you yet?
  • Yes, I've already seen you.
  • Have they been to Europe yet?
  • Yes, they have already been to Europe.
  • Has she prepared lunch yet?
  • Yes, She has already prepared lunch.
  • Have we bought the house yet?
  • Yes, we have already bought the house.

When we use the present perfect, there is a connection with now. The action in the past has a result now:

  • Tom has lost his key. (= he doesn’t have it now)
  • He told me his name, but I’ve forgotten it. (= I can’t remember it now)

When we say ‘something has happened’, this is usually new information

  • Ow! I’ve cut my finger.
  • Police have arrested two men in connection with the robbery.
